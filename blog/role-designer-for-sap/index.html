Role Designer for SAP

Posted on May 24, 2012

At GRC 2012 in Milan we will present the first results of our partnership with bay31. We worked hard in the last months on extending bay31 Role Designer for SAP environments.Â We developed several integration pieces for importing and exporting data from SAP backends:

  • Download of authorization information (user,roles,profiles,etc.) from SAP systems and upload into Role Designer. The extractor has a very good performance and enables us to download several gigabytes of data in less than a minute.
  • Import transaction usage statistics. By seeing exactly who is executing transactions compared to transactions assigned through roles we can analyze role usage levels. In Role Designer we can perform activity mining in addition to role mining to identify good candidate roles based on transaction usage patterns.
  • Export newly defined or refactored roles and upload them in SAP for further refinement.

In addition to the technical integration we worked on adapting the Role Designer methodology to the unique characteristics of the SAP environment. One of the key ideas is to enrich the purely mathematical aspects of role mining with business information. This enables the discovery of roles that “make sense” for business people. SAP has a wealth of metadata that we are integrating into the analysis:

  • Users: company codes, departments, organisational values from HR (if configured)
  • Transactions: usage types (expert users, normal users), modules (FI, CO..), other attributes (display, change, master data, customizing), criticality segregation of duties, data privacy) and many more.
  • Authorizations: module (FI,CO..), criticality, etc.

This metadata is then used for different purposes. Firstly for guiding the data mining process in finding roles that make more business sense by tuning the weighting of different parameters or by using attributes to partition the data mining space. Then also for analyzing existing roles by “calculating” role metrics.

I will cover some of these aspects in some future blogs. If you are interested and happen to be at GRC 2012 in Milan do not hesitate to come at the bay31 booth, where we can give a real-life demo. We would love to start a broader discussion with all interested parties on the benefits of role analytics (mining and metrics).

For those interested in the mathematical aspects, I highly reccomend reading some of Alessandro research papers on role mining.
